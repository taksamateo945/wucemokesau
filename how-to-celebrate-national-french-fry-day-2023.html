How to Celebrate National French Fry Day 2023

Do you love French fries? If so, you’re not alone.

Americans eat around 30 pounds of French fries per person each year1. That’s a lot of crispy, golden-brown potato slices!

But did you know that there is a special day dedicated to celebrating this delicious snack?

It’s called National French Fry Day, and it falls on July 14 in 2023.

That’s right, it’s a Fry-day!

But wait, you might be wondering, why is it on July 14? Isn’t it supposed to be on July 13?

Well, the answer is not so simple.

There are actually two versions of National French Fry Day, and they have different origins and histories.

The History of National French Fry Day

The first version of National French Fry Day is observed on July 13 every year.

This date was chosen by the National Day Calendar, a website that tracks fun, unusual, and unique national days.

According to the National Day Calendar, the term “French fries” refers to deep-fried slices or strips of potatoes that were popularized by the French in the mid-1700s.

They were sold on street carts called “frites” and were cut in various shapes and sizes.

The National Day Calendar also credits Thomas Jefferson for bringing French fries to America.

Jefferson was an ambassador to France and served “potatoes served in the French manner” at a White House dinner in 1802.

However, some people dispute this version of history and claim that French fries are not really French at all.

They say that French fries were invented by Belgians in the 17th century when they sliced potatoes like fish fillets and fried them in hot fat during winters when fishing was difficult.

They also say that American soldiers who were stationed in Belgium during World War I called them “French” fries because they spoke French, the dominant language in southern Belgium.

The second version of National French Fry Day is observed on the second Friday in July every year.

This date was proposed by Checkers & Rally’s, a fast-food chain that specializes in fries, in 2022.

They gathered online petition signatures asking the National Day Calendar to move National French Fry Day to a Friday so that it would always be a Fry-day.

The National Day Calendar agreed and made the change official.

However, not all French fry lovers have accepted this change.

Some still prefer to celebrate on July 13, while others celebrate on both days or any day they want.

How to Celebrate National French Fry Day

No matter which date you choose to celebrate, there are many ways to enjoy National French Fry Day. Here are some ideas:

Make your own fries at home. You can use a deep fryer, an air fryer, an oven, or a microwave to cook your potatoes.

You can also peel them or leave the skin on, cut them into different shapes and sizes, season them with salt, pepper, herbs, or spices, and dip them in ketchup, mayo, cheese sauce, or any other sauce you like.

Try different types of fries from around the world. For example, you can try Belgian fries with mayonnaise, Canadian poutine with cheese curds and gravy,

British chips with vinegar, Indian masala fries with curry spices, Mexican nacho fries with cheese and salsa, or Japanese furikake fries with seaweed and sesame seeds.

Visit your favorite fast-food restaurant or local diner and order some fries.

Many places offer free or discounted fries on National French Fry Day as a promotion.

For example, McDonald’s gives away free fries on July 13 if you use their app3, Burger King offers $1 large fries on July 14 if you use their app2, and Wendy’s has $1 large fries until July 31 if you order online or via their app.

Share your love of fries with your friends and family.

You can host a fry party at your home or go out to eat together.

You can also post pictures of your fries on social media with the hashtag #NationalFrenchFryDay and see what other people are eating.

Why Celebrate National French Fry Day

National French Fry Day is more than just an excuse to eat some tasty potatoes.

It’s also a way to appreciate the history and diversity of this popular snack.

French fries have been around for centuries and have traveled across continents and cultures.

They have been adapted and modified by different chefs and cuisines.

They have been enjoyed by millions of people from all walks of life.

French fries are also a source of comfort and joy for many people.
